# User Testing

DormVibe was evaluated through informal usability testing sessions involving students and staff members from the University of Nottingham Ningbo China.

---

## Participants

- Approximately 20 students and staff members participated
- 10 participants provided detailed usability feedback
- Participants included both domestic and international students

---

## Testing Objectives

The testing process focused on:

- Ease of onboarding
- Quiz completion experience
- Recommendation clarity
- Room DNA understanding
- Overall user experience

---

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Onboarding Complexity

Several users reported that the onboarding flow contained too much information early in the journey.

**Action Taken**

- Simplified onboarding screens
- Improved page hierarchy
- Reduced visual clutter

---

### Finding 2: Room DNA Engagement

Users responded positively to the Room DNA feature and found it memorable compared to traditional accommodation platforms.

**Action Taken**

- Increased visual emphasis on Room DNA results
- Improved readability of compatibility information

---

### Finding 3: Recommendation Navigation

Users occasionally struggled to understand how recommendation results connected to later pages.

**Action Taken**

- Improved navigation cues
- Added clearer page progression indicators
- Refined layout consistency

---

## Outcomes

Testing feedback directly influenced multiple interface improvements and helped validate the project's core concept of lifestyle-based accommodation matching.

The testing process also provided valuable insights into accessibility, usability, and overall product-market fit for student users.
